# Management Summary

Replica.md should launch as a focused writing and knowledge workspace before it grows into a larger platform. The strongest early version is the one that opens a folder safely, renders Markdown well, tracks relationships accurately, and gives users confidence that their notes remain portable.

## Priority for the first release

- Reliable editing, file navigation, backlinks, search, and workspace recovery come first.
- Collections, maps, publishing, mobile, and extensions should be layered on top of the index once the foundation is stable.
- The interface should feel calm and predictable rather than packed with unfinished surfaces.

## Stakeholder view

- The project can be explained as a local-first workspace with structured knowledge features.
- The technical risk sits mostly in indexing, sync, extension permissions, and cross-platform packaging.
- The documentation package gives enough detail to scope a build without locking the team into every UI detail.

## Review checklist

- The first release can be scoped from this page and the linked architecture notes.
- A reader understands what is in, what is later, and what remains unresolved.
- The summary leads naturally into the system overview and product blueprint.
